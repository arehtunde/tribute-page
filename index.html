Kobe 'Bean' Bryant

The Black Mamba

Kobe Bean Bryant (August 23, 1978 â€” January 26, 2020) was an American professional basketball player. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, Bryant entered the National Basketball Association (NBA) directly from high school and played his entire 20-season professional career with the Los Angeles Lakers.

He won many accolades including, 5 NBA championships, 2 NBA finalS MVP, an NBA MVP among others. Bryant ranks fourth on the NBA all-time regular season scoring and all-timepostseason scoring lists.

Here is a timeline of Kobe Bryant life

  • 1978: Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • 1992: Joined Lower Merion High school in Philadelphia.
  • 1996: Led his high school to the first state championship in 53 years. He averaged 30.8 points and 12 rebounds during that run.
  • 1996: Selected 13th overall in the NBA draft by the Charlotte Hornets and was subsquently traded to the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 1997: Became the youngest player to win the Slam Dunk Contest at the NBA All-Star Weekend.
  • 1998: Made first of his 18 All-Star appearances, which included 17 straight from 2000-2016.
  • 2000: Won his first of five NBA championships. Also made the first of his nine All-NBA Defense First team.
  • 2002: Named to first of eleven All-NBA first team which is tied for the most in NBA history with Karl Malone and LeBron James.
  • 2006: Scored 81 points in a game against the Toronto Raptors, which is the second-most points scored in a game in NBA history.
  • 2008: Named the NBA MVP winner, his lone NBA MVP honor.
  • 2009: Won his first NBA Finals MVP award before winning it again in 2010.
  • 2016: Retired from the NBA with a vintage performance scoring 60 points against the Jazz.
  • 2018: Bryant Animated film, Dear Basketball, which he wrote and voiced won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film and the Annie Award for Best Animated Short Subject. Dear Basketball also won a Sports Emmy Award.
  • 2020: Died in a helicopter crash along with eight others, including his daughter Gianna, in California at the age of 41.
